Welcome

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Political Science and International Relations at the University of Geneva. I spent the 2014-2015 academic year as a visiting scholar in the Department of Politics at New York University and the 2013-2014 academic year as a Visiting Student Research Collaborator (VSRC) in the Department of Politics at Princeton University.

My primary research interests include legislative institutions and behavior, political representation, formal theory, and quantitative methods. In my dissertation, I develop theoretical and empirical models to examine under what circumstances roll call votes occur in legislatures and how the use of such votes affects the voting behavior of legislators. To read more about my work, see Research. Please feel free to contact me for more information.
